About the Event

The Poster Presentation event at ACUMEN 2025 offers students an excellent opportunity to present their technical research, innovative concepts, and project findings through visually compelling posters. This format combines art and science, allowing presenters to communicate complex ideas in an accessible and engaging manner.

Unlike traditional paper presentations, poster sessions facilitate more direct interaction with the audience, creating an informal and dynamic environment for discussion. Participants will set up their posters in the exhibition area where judges and attendees can freely interact, ask questions, and provide feedback on your work.

Poster Requirements

Poster Format

  • Standard poster size: 36" x 48" (vertical orientation)
  • Digital submission of poster PDF required one week before the event
  • Physical poster to be printed and brought by participants
  • QR codes linking to supplementary materials are encouraged
  • All text should be legible from at least 1 meter distance

Content Guidelines

Your poster should include the following sections:

  • Title, authors, and institution affiliations
  • Abstract (150-200 words)
  • Introduction and problem statement
  • Methodology and approach
  • Results and findings (with visual representations)
  • Conclusions and future work
  • Acknowledgments and references

Design Best Practices

Visual Hierarchy

Organize content with clear headings and logical flow that guides viewers through your research story

Graphics & Visuals

Use high-quality images, diagrams, and graphs that enhance understanding of your research

Text Optimization

Keep text concise; use bullet points and short paragraphs with at least 24-point font size

Color & Contrast

Use a consistent color scheme (3-4 colors) with sufficient contrast for readability

Evaluation Criteria

Technical Content (35%)

Quality of research, methodology, results, and technical depth

Visual Design (25%)

Organization, layout, readability, and effective use of visual elements

Presentation (25%)

Ability to explain concepts, answer questions, and engage with the audience

Innovation (15%)

Originality of the research, creative approaches, and potential impact

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I present the same research in both paper and poster formats?
No, you must choose either the Paper Presentation or Poster Presentation format for any given research work. This ensures diversity of topics across events and prevents duplicative submissions.
Will printing facilities be available at the venue?
No, participants are expected to bring their printed posters to the event. We recommend using professional printing services for best quality. A list of nearby printing facilities will be shared with registered participants.
Are interactive or digital elements allowed in the poster?
Yes, you may include interactive elements such as QR codes linking to videos, demos, or supplementary materials. You may also bring a tablet or laptop to show digital content, but the primary presentation must be the physical poster.
What if multiple team members want to explain different aspects of the poster?
This is encouraged! Multiple team members can be present during the judging and open viewing sessions. You can decide how to divide the presentation among team members to best showcase your expertise in different areas of the research.
